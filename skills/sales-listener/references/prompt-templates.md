# Prompt Templates

Copy-paste prompts for common social listening tasks.

---

## Setup Prompts

### Define Your Monitoring List
```
I sell [PRODUCT TYPE] to [JOB TITLES] at [COMPANY TYPE].
My main competitors are [COMPETITOR 1], [COMPETITOR 2], [COMPETITOR 3].

Help me create a monitoring plan:
1. Which subreddits should I watch?
2. What G2/Capterra pages should I track?
3. What RSS feeds should I set up?
4. What keywords should I alert on?
```

### Find Relevant Subreddits
```
I sell to [JOB TITLE]s at [COMPANY SIZE] companies in [INDUSTRY].

What subreddits do they use?
Include both obvious and niche options.
List subscriber count and what's typically discussed.
```

### Set Up RSS Feeds
```
Find RSS feed URLs for these competitors:
- [COMPETITOR 1]
- [COMPETITOR 2]
- [COMPETITOR 3]

For each, find their:
- Blog feed
- Press/news feed
- Release notes (if available)
```

## Competitor Monitoring Prompts

### Weekly Competitor Check
```
Search for mentions of [COMPETITOR] across Reddit and Hacker News from the last 7 days.

Summarize:
1. Overall sentiment (positive/negative/neutral)
2. Key complaints or criticisms
3. Key praise
4. Anyone looking to switch away
5. Anything I can use in sales conversations
```

### G2 Review Analysis
```
Analyze recent G2 reviews for [COMPETITOR].

Tell me:
1. Overall rating and trend
2. Top 3 complaints with quotes
3. Top 3 praise points with quotes
4. Who's switching to them and why
5. Who's switching away and why
6. Landmine questions I can ask based on complaints
```

### Competitor Comparison
```
Compare what people say about [COMPETITOR A] vs [COMPETITOR B] on Reddit, G2, and Hacker News.

Who wins on:
- Ease of use
- Customer support
- Pricing/value
- Features

What are the key differences buyers mention?
```

### New Competitor Announcement
```
[COMPETITOR] just announced [FEATURE/FUNDING/NEWS].

Help me understand:
1. What does this mean for the market?
2. How should I position against this?
3. What questions will prospects ask?
4. What's my response?
```

## ICP Research Prompts

### Find Pain Points
```
Search Reddit and Hacker News for discussions where [JOB TITLE]s talk about [PROBLEM].

What are they frustrated with?
What solutions have they tried?
What words do they use to describe the problem?
Give me quotes I can use in my messaging.
```

### Understand Buying Criteria
```
Find discussions where people evaluate or compare [CATEGORY] tools.

What criteria do they use?
What features are must-haves vs nice-to-haves?
What makes them choose one over another?
```

### Discover Common Objections
```
Search for discussions about [YOUR PRODUCT CATEGORY].

What concerns or objections come up?
What makes people hesitant to buy?
What questions do they ask before purchasing?
```

Search Reddit for posts from the last 7 days where someone is:

## Content & Messaging Prompts

```
Find 10 Reddit or G2 quotes where people describe [PROBLEM YOUR PRODUCT SOLVES].

Give me the exact words they use.
I want to use this language in my outreach and content.
```

### Objection Research
```
When people evaluate [CATEGORY] tools, what objections come up about:
- Price
- Implementation
- Features
- Support

For each objection, give me:
1. How they phrase it
2. What's behind the concern
3. How I should address it
```

### Competitive Positioning

Based on Reddit and G2 discussions, how do buyers perceive [COMPETITOR]?

What's their reputation for:
- Product quality
- Customer support
- Pricing
- Ease of use

## Trend & Market Prompts

### Industry Trends
```
What's Hacker News and Reddit saying about [TREND/TOPIC] in the last 30 days?

Summarize:
- Main opinions and debates
- Concerns or criticisms
- Tools or solutions being mentioned
- Where things are heading
```

### New Market Entrants
```
Search for new [CATEGORY] tools that have been discussed on Hacker News or Product Hunt in the last 90 days.

List them with:
- What they do
- How they're positioned
- Community reaction
- Whether they're a threat
```

### Category Perception
```
How does the market perceive the [CATEGORY] space?

Based on Reddit and HN discussions:
- Is it seen as crowded?
- What gaps do people mention?
- What's frustrating about current options?
- What would an ideal solution look like?
```

## Weekly Monitoring Routine

### Monday Morning Scan
```
Give me a summary of the last 7 days across:

1. Reddit mentions of [COMPETITOR 1], [COMPETITOR 2], [COMPETITOR 3]
2. Any "Ask HN" posts about [CATEGORY]
3. New G2 reviews for competitors
4. Any trigger events (recommendations requests, switching discussions)

Prioritize by importance.
```

### End of Week Digest
```
Based on this week's monitoring, what are the top 3 insights I should know?

Consider:
- Competitor moves
- Customer complaints
- Market trends
- Opportunities to act on
```

## Quick Single-Purpose Prompts

**Competitor sentiment:**
```
What's the general sentiment about [COMPETITOR] on Reddit? Positive, negative, or mixed? Give examples.
```

**Find complaints:**
```
What are the top 5 complaints about [COMPETITOR] on G2 and Reddit?
```

**Recommendation threads:**
```
Find Reddit threads from the last 30 days where someone asks for [CATEGORY] recommendations.
```

**Switching discussions:**
```
Find discussions where people talk about switching away from [COMPETITOR]. What's driving it?
```

**Feature requests:**
```
What features do people wish [COMPETITOR] had? Search G2 reviews and Reddit.
```

**Pricing sentiment:**
```
What do people say about [COMPETITOR]'s pricing? Is it seen as expensive, fair, or cheap?
```

**Support reputation:**
```
What's [COMPETITOR]'s reputation for customer support based on G2 and Reddit?
```

**ICP language:**
```
How do [JOB TITLE]s describe [PROBLEM] in their own words? Find Reddit quotes.
```

## Pro Tips

1. **Be specific** - "[Competitor] complaints on G2" beats "tell me about [competitor]"
2. **Set time ranges** - "Last 7 days" keeps it fresh
3. **Ask for quotes** - Real language is more useful than summaries
4. **Request action items** - "What can I use in sales?" makes it practical
5. **Combine sources** - "Reddit AND G2 AND HN" gives fuller picture
6. **Iterate** - If results aren't useful, refine and ask again
